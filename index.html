While you wait: shut off the water

Stopping the water now saves your drywall and flooring. Here's where the main shut-off usually is:

  • Front of the house. Look for a valve on a pipe coming out of the ground near an outdoor faucet, usually on the side facing the street. Turn it clockwise until it stops.
  • At the meter. If there's nothing at the house, find the concrete box in the sidewalk or parkway near the curb. The valve inside turns a quarter-turn with a wrench or pliers.
  • Water heater leaking? Turn the valve on the cold line going into the top of the tank. If it's gas, turn the gas control dial to OFF too.

If a valve is corroded and won't move, don't force it — an old valve can snap off and make things worse. Shut off at the meter instead, and tell us when you call.
